Inpatient Hospital Data Analysis Summary

The analysis was conducted for the span from June 1, 2022 to May 31, 2023, which provided interesting and valuable insights from the year-long inpatient hospital data.

Highlights of Analysis

  • For the period in examination, we had a total of 11,091 hospital admissions.
  • The age group above 65 recorded the highest number of admissions (3340) followed closely by age groups 18 to 34, 35 to 49 and 50 to 64.
  • Gender distribution was fairly even with males accounting for 5555 encounters and females 5536 encounters.
  • Diabetes was the most prevalent medical condition followed closely by Arthritis and Cancer, all having slightly above 1800 cases.
  • Most common admission type was Elective followed closely by Emergency and Urgent.
  • Aspirin and Ibuprofen were the most common medications linked to hospital encounters.
  • The dataset presented a well balanced spread across various insurance providers - Aetna, Blue Cross, Cigna, Medicare and UnitedHealthcare
  • The total bill amount for the year-long period was $281,811,328.01

Descriptive Analysis Insights

  • The average bill amount per encounter was $25409.01 with a marginal deviation in the median at $25379.57
  • Diabetes condition showed the highest billing impact amounting to $48,305,610, followed closely by Arthritis and Cancer among others.
  • Medication-wise, the total bill amount was highest for patients taking Ibuprofen followed closely by those on Aspirin and Lipitor.

Advanced Analytic Insights

  • Testing for higher medical costs for patients with abnormal or inconclusive test results revealed that Medicare patients presented a significant rise (5.01% deviation) in mean billing amount. However, the p-value (0.048637) indicated that this result was marginally statistically significant.
  • Only weak correlation was established between age, billing amount and length of stay.

Forecasted Billing Amount

  • Forecasts for the upcoming three months (July to September, 2023) predict a slight decrease in billing amount monthly.

Areas of Attention

  • Further analysis can be conducted on the statistically significant higher spend by Medicare patients with abnormal test results to understand its causes and evaluate possible cost management strategies.
  • Continued monitoring of billing amounts per encounter across various insurance providers, medications and medical conditions is necessary to highlight potential issues and opportunities for cost reduction.
  • Understanding the weak correlation between age, billing amount and length of stay could guide admission strategies and resource allocation for better patient care and cost efficiency.
